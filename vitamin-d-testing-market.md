<p><strong>The Vitamin D Testing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>RIA</li><li>ELISA</li><li>CLIA</li><li>POCT</li><li>LC-MS/MS</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Vitamin D testing market comprises several key types: RIA (Radioimmunoassay), a sensitive method utilizing radioactively labeled antigens; ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay), which employs enzyme-linked antibodies for quantification; CLIA (Chemiluminescent Immunoassay), known for high sensitivity and speed; POCT (Point-of-Care Testing), offering rapid results at the patient's location; and LC-MS/MS (Liquid Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry), a highly accurate method for precise Vitamin D measurement. Each type caters to different clinical needs and settings in assessing Vitamin D levels.</p></p>

<p><p>The Vitamin D testing market primarily revolves around two key tests: the 25(OH) test and the 1,25(OH) test. The 25(OH) test measures the total level of vitamin D in the body, commonly used for assessing deficiency or sufficiency. In contrast, the 1,25(OH) test evaluates the active form of vitamin D, primarily utilized for diagnosing conditions related to calcium metabolism and certain disorders. Together, these tests support healthcare providers in managing bone health, immune function, and overall well-being.</p></p>
