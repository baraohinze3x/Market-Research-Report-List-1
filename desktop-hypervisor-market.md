<p><p>The desktop hypervisor market is segmented into cloud-based and on-premises types. Cloud-based hypervisors operate on remote servers, enabling users to access virtual machines via the internet, offering scalability and reduced hardware costs. In contrast, on-premises hypervisors are installed directly on local systems, providing greater control and security for organizations that prefer to manage their infrastructure. Both models cater to different business needs, with cloud solutions emphasizing flexibility, while on-premises solutions focus on data privacy and customization.</p></p>
