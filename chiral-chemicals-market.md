<p><ul><li>Traditional Separation Method</li><li>Asymmetric Preparation Method</li><li>Biological Separation Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The chiral chemicals market comprises various methods for producing chiral compounds, primarily focusing on three types. Traditional separation methods involve physical processes like fractional crystallization to isolate enantiomers from racemic mixtures. Asymmetric preparation methods utilize catalytic reactions to create one specific enantiomer, enhancing efficiency and selectivity. Biological separation methods leverage biocatalysts or living organisms to achieve enantioselectivity, providing an environmentally friendly approach. Together, these methods cater to the growing demand for chiral chemicals in pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, and specialty chemicals.</p></p>
